Beaches & Coast of Aotearoa New Zealand

New Zealand's shoreline gives you everything from quiet coves to energetic surf beaches. This page is a visitor-friendly summary inspired by the official tourism guide.

Why this coast stands out

Across both islands, the coast changes quickly—golden bays, black-sand surf breaks, rocky headlands, and sheltered spots for relaxed swimming. With so much shoreline, it's usually easy to find a beach that feels peaceful even in popular regions.

North Island highlights

  • Cathedral Cove: dramatic coastal arch and clear water views.
  • Ninety Mile Beach: a long wild coast in the Far North.
  • Hot Water Beach: naturally heated sand at low tide.
  • Piha & Raglan: iconic west-coast surf culture and bold scenery.
  • Mount Maunganui & Ōhope: easy-going beach towns for family trips.

South Island highlights

  • Tunnel Beach Walkway: cliffside tracks and ocean viewpoints.
  • St Clair & Oreti: broad beaches with room to stretch out.
  • Moeraki Boulders: unusual spherical rocks by the sea.
  • Golden Bay: calm water, warm light, and laid-back summer energy.
  • Coastwide variety: choose remote nature or active water adventures.

Simple planning tips

  • Bring sun protection year-round—the UV can be strong even when it's cool.
  • Check tide and surf conditions before swimming or exploring rocky areas.
  • Pack for quick weather changes, especially on exposed coastlines.
