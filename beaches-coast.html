Beaches & Coast of Aotearoa

Uncrowded shorelines, dramatic views, and easy coastal day trips across both islands.

Why New Zealand's Coastline Stands Out

New Zealand's long coastline makes it easy to find a beach that feels peaceful and open. From bright sand bays to rugged headlands, the coast offers places for swimming, surfing, picnics, and scenic walks.

Popular North Island Beaches

Cathedral Cove

Known for its natural rock arch, clear water, and photo-worthy coastal track.

Ninety Mile Beach

A vast stretch of shoreline in the far north, great for long walks and big-sky sunsets.

Piha & Raglan

Wild west-coast surf spots with black-sand beaches and dramatic volcanic landscapes.

South Island Coastal Highlights

Tunnel Beach & St Clair

Historic and windswept Dunedin coastlines with cliffs, waves, and walking access.

Moeraki & Golden Bay

From unusual round boulders to calm northern bays, this coast blends geology and relaxation.

Coastal Trip Tips

  • Pack sun protection and layers; beach weather can change quickly.
  • Check local conditions before swimming or surfing.
  • Keep flexibility in your plan for spontaneous stops and viewpoints.
