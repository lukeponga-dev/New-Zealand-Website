Scenery & Coastline

Beaches and Coast in Aotearoa

New Zealand offers beach days for every pace: quiet coves, family-friendly shores, dramatic surf breaks, and scenic headlands where the ocean does all the talking.

A coastline with room to breathe

The inspiration for this page comes from Tourism New Zealand's beach and coast guide. Their overview highlights how easy it is to find space by the water, with a huge variety of shores spread across both islands.

This adapted summary keeps the same travel-planning spirit while using fresh wording for this project site.

Popular North Island beach picks

  • Cathedral Cove: famous for its natural arch and turquoise water views.
  • Ninety Mile Beach: a long stretch of sand where road trips and sunsets shine.
  • Hot Water Beach: dig your own warm pool at low tide.
  • Piha & Raglan: west coast surf culture with powerful waves.
  • Mount Maunganui: an easy mix of beach time, cafés, and walking tracks.

South Island coastal experiences

  • Tunnel Beach Walkway: cliffs, sea views, and a dramatic historic tunnel.
  • St Clair Beach: classic city-meets-surf atmosphere in Dunedin.
  • Oreti Beach & Sandy Point: wide-open spaces ideal for long walks.
  • Moeraki Boulders: unusual spherical rocks on a photogenic shoreline.
  • Golden Bay: calmer waters and easy access to nature adventures.

Simple coast-day planning tips

Bring sun protection even on cloudy days.

Keep a flexible route so you can stop at unexpected viewpoints.

Check conditions and swim where patrols or local guidance are available.

Early morning and late afternoon are great for photos and quieter beaches.
